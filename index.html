Why Delta Mist Exists

Roads Age. Delta Mist Reverses It.

Asphalt doesn't fail from use—it fails from oxidation. As UV and heat break down the binder, it becomes brittle, loses flexibility, and begins to crack. Traditional preservation just seals the surface. Delta Mist® goes deeper.

Developed at the Warner Babcock Institute for Green Chemistry by Dr. John Warner—drawing on the same molecular science that informed Alzheimer's drug research—Delta Mist uses small molecules to penetrate up to 3/8" into the pavement surface, reversing oxidative damage in the binder itself, not just coating it.

The result: a treatment that doesn't just slow deterioration—it actively restores the pavement's original flexibility and cohesion, buying years of additional service life at a fraction of the cost of reconstruction.

Performance & Mechanism

How Delta Mist Works — and What It Delivers

Delta Mist operates at the molecular level to reverse asphalt aging. Here is what that means in practice for pavement managers.

3/8"
Penetration Depth — Reaches Oxidized Binder Beneath the Surface

Penetrates and Reverses Oxidative Aging

Delta Mist's small-molecule formula absorbs up to 3/8" into the asphalt matrix, interacting directly with oxidized binder—reducing stiffness and restoring original flexibility. It doesn't mask aging; it chemically reverses it.

Restores Cohesion — Delays Cracking and Raveling

By improving cohesion between aggregates and reducing brittleness, Delta Mist delays crack initiation, resists raveling and pothole formation, and blocks moisture from entering through micro-voids in the surface.

Dries Crystal Clear

Dries fully transparent in 30–90 minutes. No darkening, no discoloration of curbs or concrete, no line repainting required. Applied over existing striping with no loss of visibility.

No Sand. No Cleanup.

No aggregate cover required. No sand sweeping. No special handling equipment. Roads reopen to traffic within 60–90 minutes, minimizing disruption on high-volume streets.

100% Plant-Based and Non-Toxic

Formulated from linseed oil—a natural, renewable plant-based material. Non-toxic, non-regulated, and non-hazardous. Safe around waterways and sensitive environments.

Works on Open-Graded Friction Courses

Unlike petroleum emulsions, Delta Mist doesn't clog porous friction course voids—preserving drainage and surface friction. Suitable for bikeways and safety-critical surfaces.

Delta Mist vs. The Alternatives

Why Pavement Managers Choose Delta Mist

Every fog seal preserves the surface. Only Delta Mist rejuvenates the binder beneath it.

vs. Petroleum-Based Seals

CMS-1PF and similar asphalt emulsions darken pavement, require line repainting, leave sticky residue that tracks onto driveways and sidewalks, and discolor curbs and concrete. Delta Mist dries clear, tracks cleanly, and eliminates the repainting budget line entirely.

Standard fog seals extend life by 1–2 years at best. Delta Mist delivers 4+ years of measurable service life extension—more than double the benefit at a comparable application cost, producing the lowest Equivalent Annualized Cost in its preservation category.

The Science

Validated by NCAT at Auburn University

The National Center for Asphalt Technology at Auburn University is the gold standard for independent pavement product testing. Delta Mist has undergone the most rigorous testing regimen of any rejuvenating fog seal on the market.

After six years and 20 million Equivalent Single Axle Loads (ESALs) on NCAT's accelerated test track, Delta Mist emerged as the top-performing product in its class—and is the only product invited to continue into a third round of testing targeting 30 million ESALs.

Lab testing using the Glover-Rowe Index shows Delta Mist HR-treated aged asphalt performs 8x better than untreated asphalt at 50°F—the critical cracking temperature for cold-climate states.

The science originates from the same molecular research platform developed by Dr. John Warner at the Warner Babcock Institute. The active molecule Delta S was designed to slip between organic and inorganic phases in aged asphalt binder—disaggregating oxidized compounds and restoring original material properties.
