Why Delta Mist Exists

Roads Age. Delta Mist Reverses It.

Asphalt doesn't fail from use—it fails from oxidation. As UV and heat break down the binder, it becomes brittle, loses flexibility, and begins to crack. Traditional preservation just seals the surface. Delta Mist® goes deeper.

Developed at the Warner Babcock Institute for Green Chemistry by Dr. John Warner, Delta Mist uses small molecules to penetrate up to 3/8" into the pavement surface, reversing oxidative damage in the binder itself, not just coating it.

The result: a treatment that doesn't just slow deterioration—it actively restores the pavement's flexibility and cohesion, buying years of additional service life at a fraction of the cost of reconstruction.

How Delta Mist Works — and What It Delivers

Delta Mist works at the molecular level to address the effects of asphalt aging. Here is what that means in practice for pavement managers.

3/8"
Penetration Depth — Reaches Oxidized Binder Beneath the Surface

Penetrates and Reverses the Effects of Oxidative Aging

Delta Mist's small-molecule formula absorbs up to 3/8" into the asphalt matrix, interacting directly with oxidized binder—reducing stiffness and restoring flexibility. It doesn't mask aging; it chemically restores it.

Restores Cohesion — Delays Cracking and Raveling

By improving cohesion between aggregates and reducing brittleness, Delta Mist delays crack initiation, resists raveling and pothole formation, and blocks moisture from entering through micro-voids in the surface.

Dries Crystal Clear

Dries fully transparent in 30–90 minutes. No darkening, no discoloration of curbs or concrete, no line repainting required. Applied over existing striping with no loss of visibility.

No Sand. No Cleanup.

No aggregate cover required. No sand sweeping. No special handling equipment. Roads reopen to traffic within 60–90 minutes.

100% Plant-Based and Non-Toxic

Formulated from linseed oil—a natural, renewable plant-based material. Non-toxic, non-regulated, and non-hazardous. Safe around waterways and sensitive environments.

Works on Open-Graded Friction Courses

Delta Mist doesn't clog porous friction course voids—preserving drainage and surface friction. Suitable for bikeways and safety-critical surfaces.

NCAT Test Cycle — Invited to Continue Testing

The National Center for Asphalt Technology at Auburn University is the gold standard for independent pavement product testing. Delta Mist has undergone the most rigorous testing regimen of any rejuvenating fog seal on the market.

After six years and 20 million Equivalent Single Axle Loads (ESALs) on NCAT's accelerated test track, Delta Mist emerged as the top-performing product in its class—and is one of only two products invited to continue into a second round of testing targeting 20 million ESALs.

NCAT field cores were measured using the Glover-Rowe (G-R) Index, the industry standard for binder cracking susceptibility. A smaller G-R increase indicates slower aging and reduced cracking risk. At 60 months, Delta Mist-treated sections showed only a 123% G-R increase, compared to 347–563% for competing products—aging at less than one-third the rate of the nearest competitor over five years.

The science originates from the same molecular research platform developed by Dr. John Warner at the Warner Babcock Institute. The active molecule Delta S was designed to slip between organic and inorganic phases in aged asphalt binder—disaggregating oxidized compounds and restoring original material properties.
